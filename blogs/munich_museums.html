Museums in Munich

24th September, 2024
#Travel #Poland

Hi everyone! Today I will be talking about the museums in Munich, some tips and my favorites. I have been living in Munich for 2 years now, and I had the chance to visit every single museum in the city. I hope you find this blog helpful!

I would like to start with the bets tip you can have as a tourist, most of the museumsin Munich are only 1 euro on Sundays (not if they are not funded by the state)! Since almost every place is closed on Sundays, makes sense to visit the museums. Even though it it quite cheap and museums are one of the few options one has for something to do on a Sunday; museums are never really that crowded. So nothing should intimidate you. Also there is no need to buy your museum tickets beforehand. Here is the list of some museums that are a single euro on Sundays:

  • Alte Pinakothek
  • Neue Pinakothek
  • The Museum of Five Continents
  • Bavarian National Museum
  • The Egyptian Museum
  • Glyptothek

From the list above, my two favorites are Pinakotheks. Alte Pinakothek is such a museum that itself is an art piece. Most of the items are from between 1500-1750. But it also has a small Modern Art gallery in which you can see important pieces from Klimt, Van Gogh and Monet. Right across the street from Alte Pinakothek, stands Neue Pinakothek. It is on of my favorite contemporary art museums I have ever been in. If you are into contemporary art, then a visit there is a must.

Onto the museums that are not 1 Euro on Sundays; here is a list I named "worth your while" from most important to least (from my point of view, of course):

  • Lenbachhaus
  • Museum Brandhorst
  • Haus Der Kunst
  • Residenzmuseum
  • BMW Museum
  • Deutches Museum

Lenbachhaus

Lenbachhaus is the one museum in Munich that will display the importance if Munich in art history the most. As a fan of Kandinsky, knowing the Blue Rider art movement began in this city made me happy always. In Lenbachhaus you can see beautiful pieces from the contributers and the starters of this movement, mainly Wassily Kandinsky und Franz Marc.

Museum Brandhorst

This museum with a uniqe exterior stand in the end of Türkenstraße, where my dorm room used to be. If you like pop-art, again this museum is a must. Biggest name I can remember that is represented in the museum is Andy Warhol. If you visit this museum, and are in the are, reward yourself with a cup of tea in Brown's Tea Bar or maybe with an ice-cream from BallaBeni which is right across the museum (their coconut flavor is simply amazing).

Haus der Kunst

One has to admire this museum. It neighbors the most beautiful parts of the English Garten and has an amazing exterior. In the heart of the city, this museum was commanded by Hitler to be an art museum that displayed "real art". It is told that he despised modern art, and wanted to this museum to showcase Realism pieces that he liked only. Today, the museum stands as a contemporary art museum. Which is one of the biggest middle fingers and reasons why I love this place. And we are not done yet! On every first Thursday of each month, the museum is free to visit and open till 10 p.m. Since the exhibitions change every once in a while, this comes very useful when you live in Munich.

Others

Residenz is a unique museum, it includes parts that were built by different Bavarian kings and princes. It tires me out after a while but for some good photography it is an amazing place. BMW museum is a very good visit for an enthusiast, and a nice one for people like me, who has no particular interest in cars. Although I must admit, the joy my partner had was very infectious. And Deutsches Museum is the science museum of Munich. Unlike NEMO (the science museum of Amsterdam), the demographic of the museum are highschool students instead of elementary. The museum is next to Isar, has a wonderful view. It closes on 5 p.m. And a small tip would be that, if you go there at 4 p.m. (an hour before the museum closes), then the enterance is free.

Dachau

Once you are in Munich, it is also a good idea to visit Dachau and witness the dark side of German history first-hand. The are is free to enter for anyone who wants to visit and pay their respects.


Alright folks, that is all I have to say about museums in Munich. If you have any questions, don't hesitate to reach out. See you next time!
